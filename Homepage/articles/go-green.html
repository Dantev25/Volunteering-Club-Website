Go Green, Breath Clean !

Updated : Fri, 16 Dec 2022 | By Kholeepa Sadiyah and Ghingut Oomar | Environment

Deforestation has been one of the most prevailing ecological disaster for the past century and while major awareness is being raised on the issue, the United Nations Food and Agriculture Organization (FAO) estimates that around 18 million acres (7.3 million hectares) of forest are lost each year.The clearcutting of trees from land occur mainly in places targeted for farms, ranches, urban and industrial use and the most concentrated deforestation that was recorded occurred in tropical rainforests.

The consequences of this environmental catastrophe which include drastic climate change, increase in global warming, soil erosion, floods, wildlife extinction, acidic oceans and so on, has resulted in not only a decline in the life quality of humans globally but also to that of animals and plants.

The table below shows the top 5 countries which contributed mostly to deforestation for the last 10 years.

Country ~ Deforested Area(millions of hectares)
Brazil 42
Indonesia 27
Sudan 10
Myanmar 8
Democratic Republic of Congo 7

Source: Global Forest Assessment Score

Unfortunately, our small paradise island is no stranger to this ecological disaster. Numerous amounts of trees, especially endemic ones have been cut down for residential, industrial and urban purposes and we fear that these plant species might soon become extinct.

This is why Eco warriors club is organizing an endemic tree planting campaign which will take place on Saturday, 14th January 2023 at Vallée de Ferney.

Register to our club for more information about reaforestation programs and join us on our mission to improve our Mauritian environment.
